## Thirukkural Basics

### Structure
- **1330 Kurals** total
- **4 Sections**:
  1. Aram (Virtue/Ethics) - Kurals 1-360
  2. Porul (Wealth/Economics) - Kurals 361-1070
  3. Kamam (Love) - Kurals 1071-1330
  4. Kalaviyal (Appendix) - Prosody

### Philosophy
- **Author**: Thiruvalluvar (5th century CE)
- **Approach**: Universal wisdom + practical ethics
- **Style**: Couplet-based (2 lines per kural)
- **Depth**: Multiple interpretations at different levels

### Key Themes
- Divine/spiritual foundation
- Ethical living
- Family & relationships
- Governance & justice
- Love & devotion
- Cause & effect (karma)
- Balance & moderation

## Scene Composition Guidelines

### Visual Hierarchy
Every scene should guide viewer attention:
```
1. Primary Focus (main character/action)
2. Secondary Elements (supporting)
3. Environmental Context (background)
```

**Example**:
```
Scene 1 (Kural 1):
- Primary: Thiruvalluvar's face (wisdom, calm)
- Secondary: Sacred light manifestation
- Environment: Ancient temple, cosmic elements
```

### Color Psychology
- **Divine/Wisdom**: Gold, white, saffron
- **Nature/Life**: Green, earth tones
- **Emotion/Heart**: Red, warm colors
- **Peace/Contemplation**: Blue, cool colors
- **Transition**: Neutral grays

### Composition Patterns
- **Rule of Thirds**: Important elements at intersections
- **Leading Lines**: Guide viewer's eye to focal point
- **Depth Layers**: Foreground, middle, background
- **Symmetry**: For spiritual/sacred scenes
- **Asymmetry**: For conflict/change scenes

## Character Guidance

### Thiruvalluvar (Primary)
- **Age**: Elderly (60+)
- **Appearance**: White/saffron robes, serene expression
- **Movement**: Deliberate, measured, graceful
- **Voice**: Wise, gentle, authoritative but warm
- **Presence**: Spiritual, grounding, comforting
- **Gesture**: Palm-to-heart, open gestures, hand-over-heart

### Supporting Characters
- Vary by story, but maintain authenticity
- Respect character age & cultural roles
- Show emotional range (not just placid)
- Depict family/community relationships realistically

## Dialogue Authenticity

### Tamil Dialogues
- Use authentic Tamil (Chozha period where appropriate)
- Avoid modern slang unless contemporary setting
- Include cultural references naturally
- Respect grammatical correctness
- Maintain poetic quality (if appropriate)

**Example (Good)**:
```
"கற்றோர் அடையும் கல்விக் கடல் எம்பெருமான் என் கூற ஆம்"
(Those who have learned reach the ocean of knowledge of our Lord)
```

**Example (Avoid)**:
```
"Yo bro, I'm like totally into learning, dude!"
```

### English Translations
- Should be poetic, not literal
- Capture meaning & emotion
- Maintain rhythm where possible
- Accessible to global audience
- Scholarly but not pedantic

## Visual Metaphor Library

### Core Symbols in Thirukkural
| Symbol | Meaning | Use |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Light | Divine presence, knowledge, truth | Frequent, especially in scenes |
| Water | Life, flow, nourishment | Agriculture, change, purification |
| Fire | Passion, transformation, energy | Sacrifice, action, intensity |
| Earth/Root | Foundation, stability, grounding | Virtue, ethics, family |
| Tree | Growth, wisdom, interconnection | Life cycles, knowledge |
| Mountain | Steadfastness, obstacle, achievement | Challenges, peaks, stillness |
| River | Journey, flow, convergence | Life's path, union, movement |
| Seed | Potential, karma, growth | Cause & effect, beginnings |

### Composition Examples

**Seed/Growth Metaphor**:
- Scene opens with dried seed on barren earth
- Rain falls (visual = light + water)
- Seed sprouts, grows rapidly (time-lapse)
- Flower blooms in full glory
- Cut to character's realization

**Light Metaphor**:
- Character in darkness (confusion/ignorance)
- Light gradually enters (through window, candle, dawn)
- Light illuminates face (understanding)
- Character looks up toward light (acceptance)

## Narrative Pacing

### Scene Duration Guidelines
- **Establishing**: 0-15 seconds (introduce location)
- **Development**: 15-45 seconds (action, dialogue, learning)
- **Climax/Realization**: 30-60 seconds (aha moment)
- **Resolution**: 15-30 seconds (reflection, transition)

### Emotional Arc
```
Start (0s)
  ↓
Build tension (25% through)
  ↓
Peak (50% through)
  ↓
Reflection (75% through)
  ↓
Resolution/Message (100%)
```

## Production Feasibility

### Scene Complexity Matrix
```
EASY (1-2 locations, 1 character):
- Single character in temple
- Character with text overlay
- Simple natural scenes

MEDIUM (2-3 locations, 2-3 characters):
- Dialogue between characters
- Environmental interactions
- Moderate complexity

HARD (3+ locations, complex action, many characters):
- Crowd scenes
- Complex action sequences
- Advanced effects
```

### Feasibility Checklist
Before approving a scene:
- [ ] Location exists or can be created
- [ ] Characters available in library
- [ ] Dialogue is production-ready
- [ ] Special effects justified
- [ ] Timing realistic (not rushed)
- [ ] Technical requirements manageable
- [ ] Budget estimate reasonable

## Red Flags

### When to Request Changes

🚩 **Story Distortion**:
- Scene contradicts kural meaning
- Character behaves inconsistently
- Narrative logic broken

🚩 **Cultural Issues**:
- Inaccurate Tamil/cultural details
- Disrespectful representation
- Stereotypical portrayal
- Spiritual meaning twisted

🚩 **Artistic Concerns**:
- Visual metaphors confusing
- Pacing feels rushed/slow
- Dialogue unnatural
- Composition poorly balanced

🚩 **Production Issues**:
- Unrealistic scope
- Cost way out of proportion
- Technical specs unclear
- Timeline infeasible
